Why It Matters: The Fast Fashion Crisis

8–10%

of global carbon emissions come from the fashion industry — more than all international flights and maritime shipping combined.

Source: UN Environment Programme; Earth.org

2,700 L

of water go into making a single cotton t-shirt — roughly enough drinking water for one person for two and a half years.

Source: World Resources Institute; Earth.org

<1%

of textiles worldwide are recycled back into new clothing, even as the vast majority of garments are discarded after just a handful of wears.

Source: Ellen MacArthur Foundation; European Parliament

17M tons

of textile waste are generated in the U.S. every year, and only around 14.7% of it ever gets recycled.

Source: U.S. Environmental Protection Agency

2x

is roughly how much global clothing production has grown since 2000 — while garments are kept and worn for far less time than they used to be.

Source: Textile Exchange; Ellen MacArthur Foundation; McKinsey & Company

500,000 tons

of plastic microfibers are shed into the ocean every year just from washing synthetic clothes.

Source: Ellen MacArthur Foundation; UN Environment Programme

Figures are drawn from publicly available industry and environmental research and are updated periodically as new reports are published.
